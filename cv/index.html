Skills

Machine Learning

I have experience implementing a wide range of traditional statistical learning algorithms in my smaller-scale research projects.  The most common of these are Logistic Regression, Lasso, Support Vector Machines, and Random Forests. For my more complex projects and those with less structured data, I have implemented a range of more complex algorithms.  These include recurrent and convolutional neural networks (RNN & CNN), reinforcement learning, and autoencoder networks.

Programming

For basic statistics and supervised learning models, I use R for its simplicity and convenient modularity. For more complex scientific computing, simulation, and general-purpose work I prefer Python for its wonderful packages, particularly Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, Scikit-learn, and Jupyter. For deep learning projects, I use either TensorFlow or PyTorch. For robotics and other microcontroller / embedded system design projects, I use Embedded C. I'm always happy to learn a new language for the task at hand.

Mathematics & Simulation

Many of the systems I model use Markov Chains, Partially-Observable Markov Decision Processes, or more general frameworks. Solving a modeled system may require Linear or Convex Optimization, or a Dynamic Programming algorithm. For systems without a direct solution, or for comparison to real-world data, Monte Carlo methods provide a useful simulation framework.

Electronics

I'm proficient in using OrCAD, PSPICE, and a variety of PCB layout packages for circuit design, simulation, and layout, as well as circuit board fabrication.
